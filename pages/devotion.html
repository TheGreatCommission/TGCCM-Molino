Then he put the man on his own donkey, brought him to an inn and took care of him. 35 The next day he took out two denarii and gave them to the innkeeper. ‚ÄòLook after him,‚Äô he said, ‚Äòand when I return, I will reimburse you for any extra expense you may have.‚Äô 36 ‚ÄúWhich of these three do you think was a neighbor to the man who fell into the hands of robbers?‚Äù 37 The expert in the law replied, ‚ÄúThe one who had mercy on him.‚Äù Jesus told him, ‚ÄúGo and do likewise.‚Äù The message Jesus was trying to convey in this parable is clear for all to see: people should love everyone including their rivals. But why? There are many reasons as to why we shouldn't love everyone, especially unconditionally: maybe because the person you hate has wronged you or sinned against you - or even your friends and family for that matter. Maybe because the people you don't like are criminals. Maybe because someone has a different religious perspective, or that they don't believe in God. But let's look back to the time when Jesus did not yet die for our sins. Majority of the world back then had pretty much turned away from God, disobeying his laws and failing to keep his commandments. People turned to false gods and idols, worshipping the creations instead of the Creator, sinning without regret or guilt. Because of all this, it would not be God's fault if He had sent everyone to Hell as punishment for our sins (‚ÄúFor the wages of sin is death‚Äù, Romans 6:23). But He didn't. Instead, He sent His only son Jesus Christ, who loved us all unconditionally, and prayed for those who persecuted Him. He didn't hold any sort of animosity towards those who jeered at Him, ridiculed Him, spat at Him, or anything of the sort. And He died on the cross to make atonement for our sins. No matter how much He was rejected, criticized and made fun of by the people, He still loved us. What does this have to do with us and our enemies? In a way, His unconditional love towards us serves as an example that we should follow. No matter how disrespectful or sinful someone is - towards you or others - we shouldn't hate them for it. We should have mercy on them, similar to how God had mercy on us. People deserve a second chance. People change. When Jesus died on the cross for our sins, witnesses and disciples were spreading word about Him and His deeds. It was at this point that people realized what they were doing wrong, and changed for the better. They realized about God's unconditional love and why He sent His son to us to save us from our sins, and the penalty that is death. Let us follow this example when dealing with people we dislike or outright hate - just because they have wronged you doesn't mean that they can't change for the better. Set aside the prejudices we have and be compassionate. Let us not be like the Priest and Levite in the story who ignored the man in need - instead, let us follow the example of the Good Samaritan.
